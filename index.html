What is a brain tumor?

A brain tumor is an abnormal growth of cells in the brain or nearby structures. Some tumors are slow-growing and noncancerous; others are cancerous and grow more quickly.

Why types matter

Tumor type, grade, and location can all change symptoms, treatment, and follow-up needs. Two people with “brain tumors” may have very different care plans.

What doctors use to decide

MRI findings, surgery or biopsy results, tumor genetics, symptoms, and a patient’s overall health all help guide treatment choices.
